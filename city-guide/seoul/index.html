Seoul

dessert

Cheongsudang (청수당)

The souffle castella here was one of the best desserts I’ve had in my life. The cafe is also very aesthetic.

Baekmidang (백미당)

A chain that sells soft serve. It's pretty good, worth trying.

Cafe Haina (하인나커피)

A hidden gem that we randomly stumbled across. Their mango shaved ice is amazing and very aesthetic.

Knotted (노티드)

They had really tasty donuts. They run out quickly though, so go early.

korean food

Myeongdong Kyoja (명동교자)

Kalguksu. One of our favorite meals in Seoul. Their dumplings are also very good. Stephen says their kalguksu just tastes like Chinese 排骨汤. Yum.

Yeongdong Freshwater Eel (영동 민물장어)

The eel here is really delicious. We got the mud water eel (the most expensive thing they had), and it was worth it. They cook the eel for you in front of you.

Buchon Yukhoe (부촌육회)

Good place to try yukhoe (raw Korean beef) and moving octopus legs. I really liked it, but it's probably not for everyone.

곽만근갈비탕이야기

Really good galbitang. I was told this place is a chain, but I was not good enough at Korean to figure out where the other locations are.

Hwangsaengga Kalguksu (황생가칼국수)

I thought the kalguksoo at Myeongdong Kyoja was better, but the mandu here was better. The kimchi here is also one of the best kimchis I’ve had in Korea so far. The store workers are friendly and welcoming. We waited about 45 mins during dinner time on a Sunday.

Kim & Kim King Crab (김앤김대게)

Worth going if you like crab. The crab was delicious, but some of the other dishes were not great. We also ordered way more than we needed. They recommended 3-4kg for 4 people, but I think 2 or 2.5kg would have been a good amount for us.

Lotteria (롯데리아)

Bulgogi burgers. They taste kinda... interesting and are worth trying, imo. I liked it, and Stephen thought it was just okay.

Okdongsik (옥동식)

Dwaeji gukbap (pork rice soup). The broth and meat were refreshing and really well-done.

Tosokchon Samgyetang (토속촌 삼계탕)

This was the best samgyetang that I’ve had. The chicken was tender and perfectly cooked, and the soup was well seasoned and not too herbal.

japanese food

Sushi Kanae (스시카나에)

All the pieces were delicious, especially the signature chu-toro. We came for lunch, and the price is a steal for how much sushi you get and how high quality everything is. The chef didn’t speak much English (and we didn’t speak much Korean) but he was very kind and funny.

You need a Korean phone number in order to make a reservation, so our Korean friend made one for us. You could probably also try to ask your hotel front desk or airbnb host to make a reservation for you.

Gatten Sushi (갓덴스시)

High quality conveyer belt sushi. We went to the one in the Lotte World Mall. We waited for a while, but it was worth the wait.

Katsu Hana (카츠하나)

We were craving curry katsu, and decided to try this place. Really good.

Minami (미나미)

A little pricey, but the soba and tempura were delicious. We also tried their tuna sobamaki because it sounded weird, but it tasted pretty good.

spa

Club K Seoul (클럽K서울)

Smaller than riverside. Their co-ed area is decent.

Riverside Spa Land (강변스파랜드)

Pretty good spa. Lots of things to do.

activities

COEX (코엑스)

Huge mall full of tourists. Starfield library looks really cool. There's also an Arabica if you like coffee.

Gwangjang Market (광장시장)

Huge market with a lot of food. A good place to try a variety of traditional Korean food.

Gyeongbokgung Palace (경복궁)

We did a hanbok rental + photoshoot here. The palace is really lovely, and the pictures came out really great.

Lotte Young Plaza (롯데영플라자)

We went to a lot of malls in Seoul, but this one was probably my favorite. It has a lot of trendy-feeling Korean stores, not just high end stores.
